🔍 Issues & Service Opportunities — 911

Single-Use Plastic Oil Drain Plug (992 / 991.2)
CRITICAL ▶

The 992 and 991 Gen II both use a single-use plastic oil drain plug that must be replaced at every oil change. Reusing the plug will cause oil leaks. This is a departure from the traditional copper crush washer approach used on earlier 911s.

2020 911 992 Carrera S, 4S SIT — Oil Service Section
Consultant Tip: Always include the replacement drain plug in every oil change estimate. Stock these in the parts department — they are specific to the engine variant. Never allow a used plug to be reinstalled.
PTWA Cylinder Liner Coating — No Measurement Allowed
CRITICAL ▶

The 992 9A2evo engine uses Plasma Transfer Wire Arc (PTWA) cylinder liner coating instead of traditional liners. This coating cannot be measured with honing or bore gauges. Only visual inspection through the bore scope is permitted.

2020 911 992 Carrera S, 4S SIT — Engine Block
Consultant Tip: If any scoring or coating damage is observed during inspection, escalate immediately. This is not a field-repairable condition — engine block replacement or factory reconditioning may be required.
991 Gen II Chain Tensioner Cold-Start Rattle
IMPORTANT ▶

The 991.2 introduced the bi-turbo flat-six to the base Carrera. A known cold-start timing chain rattle exists due to reduced spring preload in the chain tensioner. The timing chain was also lengthened to 146 links vs the prior generation.

2017 911 991 Gen II SIT — Timing Drive Section
Consultant Tip: Document the rattle at every service visit with a cold-start observation note. If the rattle duration extends beyond 2–3 seconds or worsens, recommend tensioner replacement. This is a proactive upsell on high-mileage 991.2 vehicles.
Piezo Direct Injection at 200 Bar System Pressure
IMPORTANT ▶

The 992 uses piezo-electric fuel injectors operating at 200 bar system pressure. This is a significantly higher-pressure fuel system than earlier generations and requires approved service tooling for any fuel rail work.

2020 911 992 Carrera S, 4S SIT — Fuel System
Consultant Tip: Fuel system diagnostics and injector replacement must be performed with PIWIS and pressure-rated test equipment. Do not attempt manual fuel rail disconnection without depressurizing. This is a key safety and training item for technicians.
9A2evo Engine — 8.0L Oil Capacity
NOTE ▶

The 992 9A2evo engine holds 8.0 liters of oil with filter change. This is a larger capacity than many customers expect and affects the cost of synthetic oil changes.

2020 911 992 Carrera S, 4S SIT — Technical Data
Consultant Tip: Proactively communicate the 8-liter oil capacity when quoting oil changes. This justifies the higher service cost compared to competitors and earlier 911 generations. Use Porsche C40 SAE 0W-40 exclusively.
PCCB Brake Inspection Upsell
OPPORTUNITY ▶

Porsche Ceramic Composite Brakes (PCCB) require specialized measurement with the Carboteq device — standard brake gauges cannot assess ceramic pad wear. Many dealerships skip this, creating liability.

2022 718 GT4 RS SIT — Brake System (applies to all PCCB-equipped 911s)
Consultant Tip: Offer PCCB brake health inspection as a standalone service item for all PCCB-equipped 911s. Confirm your shop has the Carboteq measurement device. This differentiates your service center from independents.

🔍 Issues & Service Opportunities — 718

GT4 RS Race Oil Change Interval: 3,000 Miles
CRITICAL ▶

The 2022 718 GT4 RS SIT explicitly specifies a 3,000-mile oil change interval for vehicles used in race/track conditions. The standard 10,000-mile annual interval does not apply. Failure to follow the shortened interval under track conditions can lead to accelerated engine wear and voided warranty claims.

2022 718 Cayman GT4 RS SIT — Motorsport Service Schedule
Consultant Tip: Ask every GT4 RS and GT4 customer about track frequency at check-in. Create a dedicated motorsport service package that bundles oil change, brake fluid, and visual inspection at the 3,000-mile interval.
PCCB Brake Wear — Carboteq Device Required
CRITICAL ▶

Porsche Ceramic Composite Brakes cannot be measured with conventional pad wear tools. The Carboteq measurement device is the only approved method for assessing ceramic brake pad remaining life. Quoting brake work without this device creates liability.

2022 718 GT4 RS SIT — Brake System Measurement
Consultant Tip: Confirm your service center has a Carboteq device before accepting PCCB-equipped vehicles for brake inspection. This is a competitive differentiator — many independent shops cannot perform this service.
Brake Fluid Max 12 Months for Track Use
IMPORTANT ▶

For vehicles used on track, brake fluid must be less than 12 months old regardless of the standard 2-year interval. High-temperature track braking degrades fluid rapidly. The SIT is explicit about this shortened interval.

2022 718 GT4 RS SIT — Brake Fluid Requirements
Consultant Tip: Include brake fluid age verification in every track-use service appointment. If fluid is over 12 months old and the customer tracks the car, recommend immediate replacement before the next event.
Wheel Bearing Replacement Under Race Use
IMPORTANT ▶

Front wheel bearings should be replaced every 6,000 miles and rear bearings every 12,000 miles under race conditions per the GT4 RS SIT. These are not inspect-and-replace items for track cars — they are scheduled replacement items.

2022 718 GT4 RS SIT — Chassis / Wheel Bearings
Consultant Tip: Build wheel bearing replacement into the motorsport service schedule. Proactively quote these at the appropriate mileage thresholds. Track customers who log their events may self-report, but many do not.
986/987 Intermediate Shaft (IMS) Bearing
NOTE ▶

The 986 Boxster and early 987 Boxster/Cayman are known for intermediate shaft bearing failures that can destroy the engine. Third-party upgraded bearings (IMS Solution, LN Engineering) are a well-established preventive upgrade.

General — 986/987 Known Issue (widely documented)
Consultant Tip: This is the #1 upsell for any pre-purchase inspection on a 986 or early 987. Recommend the IMS bearing upgrade as a peace-of-mind service. Customers researching these cars already know about IMS — offering the solution builds trust.
Motorsport Service Package
OPPORTUNITY ▶

The GT4 RS SIT documents a comprehensive set of shortened intervals for track use covering oil, brake fluid, wheel bearings, wheel bolts, and visual inspections. This creates an opportunity to build a bundled motorsport service package.

2022 718 GT4 RS SIT — Complete Motorsport Schedule
Consultant Tip: Create a tiered motorsport service menu: Bronze (oil + inspection), Silver (oil + brake fluid + inspection), Gold (full SIT-recommended race service). Market directly to GT4 RS, GT4, and Spyder owners.

🔍 Issues & Service Opportunities — Cayenne

2024+ Vacuum Brake Booster System Eliminated
CRITICAL ▶

The 2024 Cayenne facelift completely removes the vacuum brake booster. An electric brake booster (EBB) is now standard. This fundamentally changes the brake bleeding and diagnostic procedure. Traditional vacuum-based bleed equipment will not work.

2024 Cayenne 9YA SIT — Brake System Architecture
Consultant Tip: Verify technician training on the electric brake booster system before accepting 2024+ Cayenne for brake service. Update SOP documentation and ensure PIWIS-guided bleeding is the standard procedure.
Electronic Wastegates (E-Wastegates)
IMPORTANT ▶

Priority Consultant Notes

R1234yf Refrigerant — SAE J2845 Required. The Taycan uses R1234yf, a flammable refrigerant. Technicians MUST hold SAE J2845 certification before performing any A/C work. This is a non-negotiable safety and liability requirement.

2025: 4 Separate Thermal Circuits. The Gen 2 Taycan has 4 independent cooling circuits (up from 2). Each requires individual inspection and fluid service. Service labor time is significantly higher than Gen 1. Source: 2025 Taycan SIT

12V Battery Drain from Connect App. Customers who frequently use the Porsche Connect app while the car is parked can drain the 12V battery. Recommend checking the 12V battery every 3 months during storage and disabling the app for long-term parking.

Heat Pump Not on All Trims. The 2025 Taycan's heat pump is standard only on select trims. Verify equipment before discussing winter range expectations with customers. Vehicles without the heat pump will have notably reduced cold-weather range. Source: 2025 Taycan SIT

Battery Conditioning: 12 min (Gen 2) vs 20+ min (Gen 1). The 2025 Taycan conditions its battery to optimal charging temperature in roughly 12 minutes, nearly halving Gen 1's time. Great trade-in talking point. Source: 2025 Taycan SIT

Charge to 85% Daily. Recommend customers use the charging planner to charge to 85% for daily use (100% for road trips only). This preserves long-term battery health — a key retention talking point for EV customers.

🔍 Issues & Service Opportunities — Taycan

R1234yf Refrigerant — Flammable, SAE J2845 Certification Required
CRITICAL ▶

The Taycan uses R1234yf refrigerant, which is flammable. SAE J2845 certification is mandatory for any technician performing A/C service. Performing A/C work without this certification creates serious safety and liability risks.

General Porsche EV Technical Requirement
Consultant Tip: Verify all technicians working on Taycan A/C systems hold current SAE J2845 certification. Maintain certification records and ensure recertification is tracked. Do not allow uncertified personnel to open the A/C system under any circumstances.
2025 Taycan: 4 Separate Thermal Circuits
IMPORTANT ▶

The Gen 2 (2025) Taycan introduces 4 independent thermal circuits with a redesigned coolant distributor, up from 2 circuits on Gen 1. Each circuit serves a different function: battery cooling, battery heating, cabin climate, and drivetrain cooling. Service complexity increases significantly.

2025 Taycan SIT — Thermal Management Architecture
Consultant Tip: Update service time estimates for 2025+ Taycan coolant service. The 4-circuit system requires individual inspection and potentially separate fluid changes. Train technicians on the new coolant distributor layout and circuit identification.
HV Battery Temperature Targets: 35°C Charging / 16°C Performance
IMPORTANT ▶

The Taycan's battery management targets 35°C for maximum DC fast charging speed and 16°C for performance driving mode. The Gen 2 battery conditioning system achieves these targets in approximately 12 minutes, nearly halving the Gen 1 time.

2025 Taycan SIT — Battery Thermal Management
Consultant Tip: Educate customers on battery pre-conditioning via the Porsche app or navigation system. Setting a charger as a destination triggers automatic pre-conditioning. This dramatically improves charging speed, especially in cold weather.
12V Battery Discharge from Porsche Connect App
IMPORTANT ▶

Customers who frequently use the Porsche Connect app (remote climate, charging status, etc.) while the vehicle is parked can inadvertently drain the 12V auxiliary battery. This can prevent the vehicle from starting or waking up.

General Taycan Technical Knowledge + Owner's Manual
Consultant Tip: Add 12V battery voltage check to every Taycan service visit. Recommend customers disable the Connect app for extended parking (>2 weeks). For long-term storage, recommend a 12V maintainer and checking battery health every 3 months.
Heat Pump — Standard on Select Trims Only (2025)
NOTE ▶

The 2025 Taycan's heat pump is NOT available on all trim levels. Vehicles without the heat pump use resistive heating, which significantly impacts winter driving range. This is a common source of customer confusion.

2025 Taycan SIT — Climate System
Consultant Tip: Verify heat pump equipment on every Taycan before discussing winter range performance. If the vehicle lacks a heat pump, set realistic cold-weather range expectations. This prevents customer disappointment and builds trust.
Gen 1 to Gen 2 Taycan Trade-In Opportunity
OPPORTUNITY ▶

The Gen 2 Taycan offers significant improvements: faster battery conditioning (12 min vs 20+), 4 thermal circuits for better management, improved range, and Porsche Active Ride suspension availability. These create compelling trade-in talking points.

2025 Taycan SIT — Complete Technical Overview
Consultant Tip: When servicing Gen 1 Taycans, mention specific Gen 2 improvements during checkout. The 12-minute battery conditioning and improved thermal management are tangible benefits customers can understand. Support service-to-sales pipeline.
Porsche Active Ride (PAR) Hydraulic Suspension
NOTE ▶

The 2025 Taycan offers an active hydraulic suspension system (PAR) that can actively raise and lower each corner independently. This system requires its own hydraulic fluid and has unique service procedures not shared with standard air suspension.

2025 Taycan SIT — Chassis / PAR System
Consultant Tip: If a customer's Taycan is equipped with PAR, flag it in the service history. PAR-equipped vehicles have additional fluid service requirements and diagnostic procedures. Confirm technician familiarity with PAR before accepting suspension work.

📜 Porsche Warranty Reference — Key Points for Service Consultants

Source: PCNA Warranty Manual 2025 (V1.0)

📅

Warranty Periods (PCNA)

  • New Vehicle Warranty4 years / 50,000 miles
  • Emissions Warranty4 years / 50,000 miles
  • Paint Warranty4 years (no mileage limit)
  • Corrosion Warranty12 years (no mileage limit)
  • HV Battery (G2/E3+)8 years / 100,000 miles
  • HV Battery (Pre-G2)6 years / 120,000 km
  • Parts Warranty2 years (no mileage limit)
  • CPO Warranty2 years / unlimited miles
⛔

Key Warranty Exclusions

  • Normal wear and tear is NOT covered
  • Expendable maintenance items (spark plugs, filters, wipers, brakes, clutches, tires) NOT covered unless defect in material/workmanship
  • Improper repair, maintenance, or care by customer or third party voids coverage for related failures
  • Non-approved modifications or tuning — voids coverage unless customer proves parts are equivalent and not the cause
  • A/C refrigerant charge only first 3 months unless part of warranty repair
  • Wheel balancing and alignment only first 3 months unless part of warranty repair
  • Improper fuel, lubricant, or fluid use
  • Rodent damage is excluded
  • Overspeed events void engine/drivetrain coverage
Critical: All service intervals MUST be completed per Porsche AG requirements as a precondition for ANY warranty claim. Missed services = denied claims.
📝

Claim Submission Rules

  • 15-Day Rule: Claims must be submitted within 15 days of the technician's last time punch
  • Repair Over Replace: Repair must be given precedence over replacement unless repair costs more
  • Exchange Parts Required: Factory exchange parts must be used on any part listed in PET with an exchange number
  • PCSS Documentation: All repairs must be fully documented in PCSS for quality analysis
  • Warranty Type 111: Use for all New Vehicle Warranty claims
  • Warranty Type 1E1: Use for Emission Warranty claims after NVW expires
⚡

EV / Hybrid Battery Warranty

  • BEV Battery Capacity: Warranty case if net capacity drops below threshold during warranty period
  • Measurement: Must be performed at an authorized Porsche Center via PIWIS
  • Exclusions: Battery removed from vehicle and opened improperly, or charging/care instructions not followed
  • Charging Equipment: If ordered as I-Option, claim under NVW. If Tequipment, claim as Parts Warranty
  • Dealer DC Charge Box: 8-year warranty period
  • AC Wall Box: Covered under standard warranty terms
Consultant Note: Battery capacity measurements are a key customer touchpoint. Proactively offer annual capacity checks to EV customers — this builds trust and catches warranty-eligible degradation early.
🎨

Paint & Corrosion Warranty

  • Paint: 4 years, no mileage limit — covers defects in paint application
  • Corrosion: 12 years, no mileage limit — covers perforation corrosion (rust-through)
  • Exclusions: Stone chip damage, environmental damage, chemical contamination, improper care, aftermarket paint
🛠

Goodwill & Special Cases

  • Goodwill: Available for out-of-warranty repairs at PCNA discretion — submit via PRMS
  • CPO 111-Point Check: Required for Certified Pre-Owned warranty activation
  • Consequential Damage after Campaigns: Covered if damage resulted from the campaign condition
  • Trickle Charging: Required for vehicles in inventory — battery damage from discharged state may not be covered
